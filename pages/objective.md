## Objective

This workflow provides guidance on the data management aspects of eDNA research.
It can help both individual analyses and entire research projects to manage data effectively.
It can also be used to introduce new staff members to the process.

This workflow is complementary to a Data Management Plan (DMP) and is intended to be appended as an annex to the DMPs of projects that follow this workflow.

The steps in the workflow are important for all people involved in eDNA research.
Who carries out which checkpoint must be agreed upon among team members, ideally before the research begins.

## Concept

When writing up a project proposal or research idea, that is also the perfect time to start your DMP.
Many of the steps that need to be taken to organise data management also form part of a DMP, and the earlier you start, the easier and smoother things will go later on.

The process from sampling to output/publication is divided into six steps:

1.**Preparation**: Ideally, before the project begins.

2.**Sample collection**

3.**Lab analyses**: The steps to go from sample to raw data.

4.**Bioinformatics**: The steps to go from raw data to "operational data", for example, an Operational Taxonomic Unit (OTU) table, and Amplicon Sequence Variant (ASV) table, or from droplets to eDNA concentrations.
This step is specific for (e)DNA research and depends on the type of analysis.

5.**Data processing**: The steps to go from "operational data" to an answer for a research question.

6.**Output/data publication**

After each step in the eDNA data workflow, we propose a **data checkpoint**, with a list of questions/actions that must be carried out before the process moves to the next step.
The intention is for one or more team members to take responsibility for carrying out each checkpoint.
The same person can carry out all checkpoints, but that is not required.
