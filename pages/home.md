Welcome to the eDNA workflows website! This website is dedicated to providing information and resources for researchers working with environmental DNA (eDNA) and metabarcoding workflows. 

eDNA metabarcoding is a powerful tool for biodiversity monitoring and conservation, allowing researchers to detect and identify species from environmental samples such as water, soil, or air. However, developing a robust eDNA workflow is not an easy process - a lot of choices have to be made and the workflow is not always straightforward. This website aims to provide a comprehensive overview of the eDNA workflow we are developing at the [Research Institute for Nature and Forest](https://www.vlaanderen.be/inbo/en-gb/homepage/) (INBO), from sample collection to data publication, and to share best practices and resources for researchers in the field. We hope that this website will serve as a valuable resource for researchers working with eDNA and metabarcoding who are concerned about open data and open science. 
Although other DNA-research is not the focus of this workflow, many of our steps can also be used in other DNA-research workflows. We hope to expand on this in the future, but of course first we need to finish the eDNA-focused workflow. Watch this space!

We welcome feedback and contributions from the community.
